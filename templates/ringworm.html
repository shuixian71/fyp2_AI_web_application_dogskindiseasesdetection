Ringworm

Ringworm is an infection of skin, hair, or claws caused by a type of fungus. In dogs, about 70% of ringworm cases are caused by the fungus Microsporum canis, 20% by Microsporum gypseum, and 10% by Trichophyton mentagrophytes Most cases of ringworm are spread by contact with infected animals or contaminated objects such as furniture or grooming tools. Apart from that, broken hairs with associated spores are important sources for spread of the disease.

Symptoms

Bald, scaly and circular patches with broken hairs and may also develop acne-like bumps on the skin.

Most common sites affected by ringworm are the face, ear tips, tail, and feet

Fragile, easily broken hair shafts that typically do not itch

Brittle, rough claws, Inflamed or scabbed skin

Prevention

Use the medicated shampoos and dips to treat the ringworm

Use diluted bleach to clean the pet's environment

Consult with veterinarian to obtain information about any treatment that may be appropriate for your pet

Oral medications are needed in longterm or severe cases
