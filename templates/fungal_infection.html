Fungal infection

Some fungal infections in dogs are contagious to humans and other animals. Fungal infections occur when fungi invade a dog’s body, either externally or internally. These infections fall into two main categories which are superficial (affecting the skin or mucous membranes) and systemic (affecting internal organs). Common types of fungal infections in dogs include , yeast infections (fungal dermatitis), and systemic infections like blastomycosis, cryptococcosis, and aspergillosis. It could be cause the environmental factor as yeast thrives in hot and humid conditions, making such climates a risk factor for developing fungal skin infections in dogs.

Symptoms

Hair loss in the affected areas, especially noticeable around the ears, armpits, and anal region

Thickened skin with elephant-skin-like appearance

Flaky, crusty, or scaly skin around the paws, nails, neck, and other sensitive areas

Thickened skin with elephant-skin-like appearance

Redness and inflammation, foul-smelling sticky discharge, particularly from the ears

Prevention

Always clean the pet's environment and make sure it is free from contamintation

Regular grooming to removes dirt, debris, and dead hair that can cause blockages in your pet's pores

Always make sure the pet is getting the proper nutrition
