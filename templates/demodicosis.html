Demodicosis

Canine demodicosis is an inflammatory condition of the skin caused by increased numbers of Demodex spp mites that are normally present in low numbers in the hair follicles and sebaceous glands.

Symptoms

Characterized by hair loss and the presence of dandruff

Red patches or simply reddish areas appear on the skin. May be localized in certain parts of the dog's body, such as around the lips (alopecia) and face, or spread over the entire skin surface

Skin redness, skin scaling, hair loss and bald spots

Skin lesions and sores along with foul odor that coming from the skin lesions

Prevention

Consult with veterinarian to obtain information about any treatment that may be appropriate for pet based on the severity

Use benzoyl peroxide shampoos, dips, anti-parasitic medications, topical ointments to fight against demodicosis

Medication such as antibiotics to treat bacterial infections
